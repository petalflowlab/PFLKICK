What is Petal Flow Labs?

"Two years of flow. One big missing piece. About 140 of them, actually — 140 more LEDs. This is the next evolution of the Petal Staff, and the birth of the Petal Flow Sword."

Petal Flow Labs builds motion-reactive LED flow props with a twist — mechanical petals that physically open and close based on your movement. No buttons, no presets. Just spin faster and they bloom. Slow down and they fold back in.

The original Petal Staff has been in active use for over two years — at festivals, parks, and late-night sessions — through drops, contact abuse, and everything in between. It works. Now it's time to do it properly.

This Founders Edition is a small, carefully managed batch. No factory minimums, no compromises. Every prop is hand-assembled — I balance and test each one personally. Because volume is low, you get something rare: a say in what you receive.

How it Works — The Technology

Every Petal Flow prop runs on a custom-designed PCB at its core. Inside the capsule: a 3-axis accelerometer, a gyroscope, a microphone, Bluetooth, and a microcontroller — all talking to the LED strip in real time.

🌸
Mechanical Petal Bloom
Petals open with centrifugal force as you spin, close when you slow down. No buttons — just movement. Works in every style from contact to freestyle flow.
📡
Real-Time Motion Tracking
The gyro + accelerometer reads your exact speed, tilt, and rotation. LEDs respond to what you're actually doing, not a pre-programmed sequence. The available detection for special effects include roll, stab, orientation, swing, and stop detection.
🎵
Sound Reactive Mode
Built-in mic syncs the LEDs to the music around you. Beat drops trigger full-strip flashes and reactive waves. Dial sensitivity from the Bluetooth app.
📱
Bluetooth App Control
Customize color palettes, sensitivity, and switch between 32+ movement & color modes wirelessly. App connects in seconds, no account needed.
Motion-reactive LED strip responding to live movement — gyro tracking in action

32 Movement & Color Modes

The firmware currently ships with 32 special modes — from martial arts combat sequences and fluid contact rolls to full beat-synced music modes. These include:

  • Swing detection — bright flares on impact and directional strikes
  • Roll & spin modes — rainbow rushes and lightning trails triggered by rotation speed
  • POV mode — pulsing sine-wave patterns that come alive during fast helicopter spins
  • Music reactive — bass, mid, treble, and beat detection all mapped to distinct LED behaviours
  • Idle breathing — a smooth wave animation when you're at rest

Development Diary — The Thought Process

Transparency is core to this project. Building a motion-blooming set of flow props isn't simple. Here is a look directly into my development diary — the real engineering trade-offs I've navigated, the iterations along the way, and the work-in-progress items I want backers to understand:

🔋 Battery Size

Final battery spec isn't locked. Current builds exceed 12 hours. Moving to a two-battery design allows smaller cells while keeping life identical — we're powering fewer LEDs per board. Many considerations need to be weighed so target life will be a range of 6 to 12 hours.

🛡️ Staff Rigidity

Polycarbonate has natural flex. Extra-long tube overlaps keep it minimal. Carbon fibre centre section gives extra stiffness while losing the leds in the middle.

⚡ Electronics

The capsule has gone through rigorous impact tests without an issue. The biggest issue will be fixed with a more rigid attachment method for the led Strip.

🎨 Mode Readiness

Firmware is functional but not final. All motion detection is working well and each mode needs optimisation and per-prop sensor calibration. This will be performed last before shipping.

These are the concerns I've found and I'm sharing them with you. No hidden issues, no glossy promises. If you have any other questions at all — please ask me directly.

About the Creator

🌸

Petal Flow Labs — Solo Founder & Builder

I've been a flow artist for five years. I picked up poi and contact staff in the mountains of Pai and on the beaches of Koh Phangan, then spent years developing my style on the streets and stages of Prague. I gravitate toward a dance-first approach — props that move with the body rather than around it.

I've always made my own props — starting with fire, then moving into LED for the versatility it opens up. The petals started as a purely visual idea. What I didn't expect was how much they'd change the feel of the flow itself — the way they behave mid-spin opened up a whole new dimension of movement. Suddenly I had options: lean into the softness the petals bring, or snap back into a tighter technical style. That ability to switch between modes mid-flow is something I hadn't experienced with any other prop, and it's what kept me developing them across poi, staff, and swords for the past two years.

The staff has come the furthest — two years of feedback, iterations, and late nights with prototypes. This is a solo project at its core, shaped along the way by generous technical input from friends and professionals who believed in it. This Kickstarter is where that work lands.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I customise the length or weight?

Yes — this is one of the perks of a low-volume founder's run. I've tested the specs I think work best, but your flow is your flow. Length and weight bias can be adjusted within reason. Message me before the campaign ends.

What are the Juggling Triples?

Club-sized swords tuned to juggling — speed sensing, orientation detection, pattern inputs. A 3-piece set designed for professional flow jugglers.

Do the props work without the app?

Yes. All modes run standalone. The Bluetooth app lets you switch modes and tweak settings, but the prop works out of the box from the moment you pick it up.

What about shipping outside Canada?

International shipping is available. Costs vary by region and will be confirmed with each backer individually. I'll cover any import/duties surprises where I reasonably can.

When will the Petal Poi ship?

Poi is still in final prototyping. It requires more refinement and testing before it can ship. Staff and Sword ship first from this batch — Poi will follow in a subsequent wave once it meets the same quality standard.
